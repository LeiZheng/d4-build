Crafting workflow per slot — affix-hunting cheatsheet

Use this as the loop you run at every gear upgrade. Items go through 4 stages: loot/gamble → reroll → temper → masterwork.

  1. Loot or gamble an item with the right slot at item-power 800 or higher.
  2. Inspect rolls. Keep if it has at least 2 affixes from the slot's top-3 priority list and one socket. Otherwise salvage and try again.
  3. Reroll at the Occultist (60-80M gold per slot is normal) — target the 4th affix to match the priority list.
  4. Temper at the Blacksmith using the recommended manuals.
  5. Masterwork to +12 — every 4th rank (4, 8, 12) rolls a +25% to a random affix. Use these to amplify the priority target.
  6. Greater Affix (gold-bordered) on a top-priority stat is worth ~5x — keep an eye out and don't reroll those off.

Heads-up on the 'Crit is king' assumption. In current Diablo IV, total damage is computed roughly as base * (1 + ADDITIVE_TOTAL) * VULN[x] * CRIT[x] * OVERPOWER[x] * SKILL_TAG[x] * CONDITIONAL[x]. Crit is one fixed multiplicative bucket among several. Whether Crit is actually the biggest contributor depends entirely on how saturated each of the other multiplicative buckets is in your build — and in most meta builds, Vulnerable and skill-tag multipliers contribute more than Crit because they're easier to stack to high values.

For this Dread Claws Warlock build, the dominant damage bucket is likely Vulnerable [x]. That means: when comparing affixes/aspects/paragon nodes, prefer ones that add to that bucket over ones that add to already-saturated buckets. Pure +X% additive damage rolls add to a bucket that's typically already at +500-700% in endgame; a single +20% in a starved multiplicative bucket can be worth more than a +60% additive roll.

Class Mechanic — Soul Shards (unlock at lvl 15)

At level 15, you can unlock your class mechanic called Soul Shards, with a secondary option called Fragments at level 30.

Mastermind Shard unlocks Laalish for free but more importantly grants synergies for Abyss and Shadowform (generated by Nether Step , Sigil of Subversion ), boosting most of our damage sources.

Blasphemous Fragment further builds into that by allowing Rampage - Abyssal Titan to apply Hex , amplifying all Abyss damage even further.

Campaign route

For your first character in Diablo 4, completing the base & VoH Campaigns provides all the background lore but is not necessary. The original Campaign is the slowest way to level, but it's worth experiencing Diablo 4's storyline at least once. The Vessel of Hatred campaign offers a much faster leveling experience and is also recommended at least once to see the story. Both legacy campaigns can be skipped if you want to jump straight into the latest content. The Lord of Hatred campaign is required to unlock War Plans and the full campaign skip for future playthroughs. For a faster pace, stay on Normal difficulty. To play Seasonal Content , you often need to have completed or skipped the Campaign. After finishing the campaign, you should focus on War Plans activities such as Helltides , Undercity of Kurast , or the Pit . Strongholds provide a significant boost of experience for little time investment. Make sure to clear all of them after around level 60.

Seasonal / Alt route

If you have never done so, first complete the Lord of Hatred campaign. If available, begin with the Seasonal Questline in order to progress your Season Rank to unlock additional perks, loot, and work on your Seasonal Perks. Preferably, stay on Hard difficulty or above to benefit from the extra XP multipliers as long as your clear speed remains fast. Use power jumps (e.g. key skill unlocks, legendary drops) to bump up the difficulty. From level 1-70, you should focus on earning experience in War Plans activities to work on your activity trees. Ideally, focus on Helltide , Pit , and Undercity objectives, as well as Infernal Hordes and Nightmare Dungeons for a fast and smooth experience. Strongholds provide a significant boost of experience for little time investment. You can clear all of them after around level 60 to speed things up. If you're playing an Alt, make sure to use Tempers and Imprints as soon as you acquire rare gear to crank up the difficulty. Find the most efficient way of leveling your character in detail with our Speed Leveling Guide .

Tips & tricks

Imprint Important Aspects on Amulet Ideally, put your best Offensive Aspect on your Amulet, since it is usually not replaced as often as other gear. Compare Your Items Press Shift to activate item comparison. Use this build guide's Stat Priorities section to identify potential upgrades. Choose an Easy Difficulty Ideally, start on Normal or Hard difficulty for the extra XP modifier, then try to go higher when it's way too easy. If that slows you down noticeably, it's better to stay on a lower difficulty. Use Your Materials There's no point in holding on to your gold or materials for later. Use everything to speed up your journey to 70. If you're twinking, make sure to temper and imprint your gear with powerful options to make leveling a breeze.

Speedrun route

If you're looking to min/max your time spent on the way to 70 instead of following a generic guideline, you need to use a specific strategy.
